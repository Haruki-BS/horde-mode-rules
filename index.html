Army Construction

In 40K Horde Mode, decide on a game size (1,000 or 2,000 points) and the number of players...

Steps to Construct Players’ Armies:

  • Divide your total points equally among players, rounding down.
  • Each player’s army must include a Warlord and follow 10th Edition rules.
  • No enhancements may be taken; they are purchased during the game.

Table Setup

  1. Choose a Deployment randomly from the Leviathan or Pariah Nexus Mission Packs.
  2. Place objective markers as per the mission pack's rules.
  3. Create and mark Horde Spawning Zones based on the game size.
  4. Set up the board with terrain only in No Man’s Land.
  5. Shuffle the Misery Deck, Secret Objectives Deck, and Secondary Mission Deck.
  6. Decide if the game will be 5 rounds or infinite.

Gameplay

40K Horde Mode uses the core rules with these alterations:

  • Players move through phases together and activate units in any order.
  • Players act as a single team. Their units count as friendly.
  • Players start with 2 CP and track them individually.
  • The Horde has the first turn but does not use Stratagems or army-wide rules.
  • A Resupply Step is added after the Battle-Shock Step in the Command Phase.

Spawning the Horde

  1. Choose a Horde Spawning Zone.
  2. Remove any units within the Spawn Zone.
  3. Roll 2D6 to determine the spawn bracket.
  4. Select a unit from the rolled bracket and place it in the Spawning Zone.
  5. Repeat for each remaining zone.

Horde AI

Movement

Horde units move toward the closest visible enemy, objective, or defender board edge, in that order.

Actions

  • Advance if advantageous.
  • Shoot at the closest target or prioritize specific keywords (e.g., Leader, Anti-X).
  • Charge if equipped with melee weapons stronger than Close Combat Weapon.

Winning & Losing

If the game is not infinite mode, it ends after 5 rounds. Players win individually by completing their Secret Objectives.
